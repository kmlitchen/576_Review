## The purpose of this web application is to enable users to record and locate structural pieces of art – such as sculptures, statues, and art installations – in public places.  

This application will display to users the variety of structural artforms around them and allow them to contribute to their community.  Many pieces of structural art are tucked away in small parks or the corners of public buildings, and this app allows users to log art that few people usually notice, so that others may know about and enjoy them.

A ***sculpture*** is simply a 3-dimensional, standalone piece of art that viewers can view from many sides, while a ***statue*** is a sculpture of a human or animal (if the statue only shows the head and shoulders of the figure, it is called a bust).  In contrast, an ***art installation***   * is an immersive piece of art (often designed for a specific space) that viewers can often walk through or interact with.  

When users go to the site, they will see a map, information explaining the site's purpose and location parameters, and a list of other known sites within the map view.  The map will have a “find my location” button so users can geolocate themselves.  They can place a pin on the map to show a known structural piece of art, and it will ask them for information about the artwork and the location where they found it.

If a user clicks/presses one of the pieces of art on the map, they will be provided with user-submitted information about it (the artist’s name, the name of the piece of art, the type of piece it is, a short description of the piece of art, and the year it was completed).  When a user submits a site, the location and art information are stored in a database.
